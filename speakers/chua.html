Amy Chua

Amy Chua is the John M. Duff Jr. Professor of Law at Yale Law School. She is the author of Battle Hymn of the Tiger Mother, a memoir about her experience raising children the traditional Chinese way, which sparked a worldwide conversation about parenting, culture, and achievement.

Chua's other works include World on Fire and Day of Empire, which explore how market economies and ethnic diversity interact, and how hyperpowers rise and fall throughout history. She is one of the most thought-provoking voices in contemporary debates on culture and society.
